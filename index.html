The course project we are working on aims to design a sustainable city that can host all the necessary facilities, contributing to its economic and social development. This includes ensuring effective urban planning, optimizing infrastructure for industrial expansion, healthcare, education, and waste management. The project also focuses on preserving the rich cultural heritage of the city while promoting tourism, all while enhancing the living standards and ensuring environmental sustainability for future generations.

In this project, the principles of Design and Analysis of Algorithms (DAA) are applied to optimize various systems within the city. For example, algorithms like Dijkstraâ€™s and Kruskalâ€™s are used for efficient road and transportation network design, while dynamic programming and greedy algorithms help in optimizing resource distribution, waste management, and healthcare delivery. Additionally, data structures like graphs and trees are leveraged for managing city data, ensuring smooth operations and decision-making processes.

The designed city is assumed to be situated in the state of Rajasthan, India, with an area of approximately 1423 square km area. The city is called Chitranagari which translates to 'City of Art'. Just like it's name, the city offers its visitors immense cultural heritage and diversity in the form of museums, forts, and palaces. It also hosts recreational sites such as an amusement park, zoological and botanical gardens. The city is well connected with roads and highways on the outskirts, supported by metro within the city. There are two government offices (North and South hubs) for smooth working. These, along with other details, make this city sustainable and one of the major economically driving cities.
